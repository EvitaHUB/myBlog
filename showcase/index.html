My DH Favorites

2020-12-11 — Written by Evita

Photo by Joshua Golde on Unsplash

Hetre I would like to refer to my favorite DH web projects. I love history, so they probably will be about history related themes. And at the moment they are more about Latvian initiatives. So for the starter :

Latvian Hillforts

My absolute favorite is one from Latvian citizen science. It is a map - guide - repository of publicly available data on Iron to Bronze age hillfort locations in Latvia. Project is called Latvijas Pilskalni or Hillforts of Latvia. It is made with lot of love and effort from few enthusiasts and used by many as valuable source of information. As hillfort locations often are in areas covered by forest and hard to notice, this resource has created 3D LIDAR based models of every hillfort. It gives explicit information on shape of the hillfort. It helps to locate them in the nature. For example this one on the picture below was discovered just 2 years ago and is in very good condition, fully covered with forest. more about the hillfort in website

Ciltskoki

Is a crowdsourcing web page with transcribed documents of digitalised archives related to geneology research records - census, metrics, passport data etc. Documents are manually transcribed to excel data sheets and published for search purposes in Ciltskoki.lv repository.

It looks rather simple, but it contains treasures - more than 3 million searchable data. It is voluntary work of few hundred people. OCR tools for Latvian handwriting are not promised for near future, so this source is highly valuable. And I wish it grows. Only in Latvian at the moment which is not helpful for those who search for their Latvian ancestors from abroad.

I am contributing to the project transcribing of church registration books of 19-20 century from Dundaga parish, where biggest part of my ancestors come from. So far about 6000 records and thats about half now.
